But What Does Your Best Life Look Like?

It's when you make 'healthy' choices, based off the data you know, after making sure it is foolproof.
It's when you treat others with respect even with their problems, or morality issues, or experiences, or past actions.
Because let's be real here, saying nobody is perfect carries a lot more weight than just 'let them get away with it' (which you don't do, because they will just do it again),
it's knowing you have issues as well, and not acting like you don't. That is the key to living your best life.
I've faced a lot of hardship due to specific people, who think I have gone through nothing for various reasons, but it's time for me to stop holding that against them and grow up.
The way my dad puts it: most adults have the minds of children, not because of their intellect, because of how their emotions control their lives.
